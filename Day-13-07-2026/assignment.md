###### Threat Actor Dossier – Lazarus Group

###### 

###### Threat Actor: Lazarus Group

###### 

###### 1\. Who They Are and Their Targets

###### 

###### Lazarus Group is a well-known Advanced Persistent Threat (APT) group that is widely assessed by governments and cybersecurity researchers to be linked to North Korea. The group has been active since at least 2009 and is known for conducting sophisticated cyber operations around the world.

###### 

###### Common Targets:

###### 

###### Financial institutions (banks and cryptocurrency exchanges)

###### Government agencies

###### Defense and military organizations

###### Healthcare organizations

###### Technology companies

###### Critical infrastructure

###### Aerospace and research organizations

###### 

###### The group has conducted operations across Asia, Europe, North America, and other regions, making it one of the world's most active cyber threat actors.

###### 

###### 2\. Primary Motive

###### 

###### Lazarus Group has multiple objectives:

###### 

###### Financial Theft: Stealing money from banks and cryptocurrency platforms to generate revenue.

###### Cyber Espionage: Collecting sensitive government, military, and technological information.

###### Disruption and Destruction: Conducting destructive cyberattacks against selected targets.

###### 

###### Unlike many cybercriminal groups that focus only on financial gain, Lazarus is believed to support broader state objectives in addition to raising funds.

###### 

###### 3\. Three Famous TTPs (Techniques, Tactics, and Procedures)

###### 1\. Spear Phishing

###### Sends highly targeted phishing emails with malicious attachments or links.

###### Tricks victims into opening malware or revealing credentials.

###### Frequently used as the initial access technique.

###### 2\. Exploiting Public-Facing Vulnerabilities

###### Exploits unpatched VPNs, web applications, or other internet-facing services.

###### Gains unauthorized access before organizations install security updates.

###### 3\. Living off the Land (LotL)

###### Uses legitimate operating system tools such as PowerShell, Windows Management Instrumentation (WMI), and Command Prompt to move through networks and avoid detection.

###### This technique reduces the need for custom malware and blends malicious activity with normal system administration.
