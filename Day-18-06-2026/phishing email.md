# \# Checklist for phishing emails

##### &#x20; Assignment

##### &#x20;  Create a Phishing Awareness Checklist: Draft a 1-page PDF checklist designed for a non-technical person (like a grandparent

##### &#x20;  or an HR employee). List 5 things they must check before clicking a link or downloading an attachment in an email.



###### &#x20;  Ans) 🛑Before You Click or Download:

&#x20;

###### &#x20;    1. **🔍 Verify the Sender :**

###### &#x20;                  **>** Check the actual email address, not just the display name. Scammers often use addresses that look legitimate but have  

###### &#x20;                    slight misspellings or use weird domains (eg., support@microsoft-support.com instead of @microsoft.com).



###### &#x20;    **2.🔗 Hover Over Links :**

###### &#x20;                    > Never blindly click a link. Hover your mouse over the link (without clicking) to preview the destination URL. Ensure the

###### &#x20;                      web address is exactly what you expect and does not contain strange characters or misspellings.



###### &#x20;    **3. 📄 Inspect Attachments :**

###### &#x20;                    > Be highly suspicious of unexpected attachments, especially .zip, .exe, or Office documents (.doc, .xlsx) that you weren't

###### &#x20;                      expecting—even if the email comes from someone you know. Confirm with the sender via phone or chat before opening.



###### &#x20;    **4. 🚨 Check the Tone and Urgency :**

###### &#x20;                    > Does the email use fear, threats, or high-pressure language? Common red flags include claims that "your account will be

###### &#x20;                      closed" or demands for immediate action. Pause and double-check through official channels before acting.



###### &#x20;    **5. ⚠️ Look for General Oddities :**

###### &#x20;                    > Scammers use generic greetings like "Dear Customer" instead of your name. Also, watch out for unusual requests, like an

###### &#x20;                      executive asking you to buy gift cards or a bank asking for your password via email.



###### &#x20;    **6. Check on website :**

###### &#x20;                    > Google Safe Browsing: https://transparencyreport.google.com/safe-browsing/search

###### &#x20;                    > VirusTotal: https://www.virustotal.com/gui/home/upload
