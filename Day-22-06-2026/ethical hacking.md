## \#Ethical Hacking:

###### &#x20;          > Ethical hacking is the authorized practice of probing computer systems, networks, and applications to uncover security vulnerabilities that malicious hackers could exploit. Also known as "white-hat hacking" or "penetration testing," it uses the exact same tools and tactics as cybercriminals, but with legal permission to strengthen defenses.



### \#How its work:

###### &#x20;  Ethical hackers operate through a strict, structured methodology to ensure systems remain safe and compliant:

###### &#x20; > Reconnaissance: Gathering technical information and mapping the target environment.



###### &#x20; > Scanning: Identifying open ports, exposed services, and configuration flaws.

###### &#x20;

###### &#x20; > Vulnerability analysis: Cross-referencing open ports with know exploits and cve.



###### &#x20; > Exploitation: Safely simulating attacks to validate weaknesses and measure potential business impact.

###### 

###### &#x20; > Post-exploitation: TRY to gain privilege escalation, lateral movement, proving impact.

###### &#x20;

###### &#x20; > Reporting: Documenting findings and delivering actionable recommendations to patch and secure the system.





#### 1)Reconnaissance:

###### &#x20;       > Reconnaissance (recon) is the preliminary phase of ethical hacking. It involves gathering actionable intelligence about a target, such  

###### &#x20;         as IP addresses, network infrastructure, and employee details. The primary goal is to map the attack surface and identify potential  

###### &#x20;         vulnerabilities before launching an authorized penetration test.



#### \#Types of reconnaissance:

###### &#x20;  It is mainly divided into two parts as how hacker interact with target.



##### &#x20; 1. Passive Reconnaissance:

###### &#x20;     > This method involves gathering information indirectly without directly interacting with the target's infrastructure. Because there is no     

###### &#x20;       direct contact, it remains completely stealthy and leaves no footprint on target logs.

###### 

##### &#x20; 2. Active Reconnaissance:

###### &#x20;     > This method involves direct probing of the target system to unearth precise, up-to-date intelligence. While it yields highly detailed 

###### &#x20;       information, it is trade-off for stealth, as it can trigger security alarms and leave traces in network logs.



##### \# Common tool uses:

###### &#x20;    > WHOIS Lookups: Querying domain registration information to find administrative contacts and IP allocations.

###### &#x20;    > Shodan: Scans the entire internet daily to index publicly exposed IoT devices, servers, open databases, and routers.

###### &#x20;    > Nmap (Network Mapper): The industry standard for network scanning. It pings addresses to discover live hosts, open network ports, active 

###### &#x20;                             services, and operating systems. 

###### &#x20;    > Wireshark: Intercepts and parses physical network traffic in real-time, giving hackers insight into protocol structures, unencrypted  

###### &#x20;                 text, and network conversations.

###### &#x20;    > DNS lookup: Deep analysis of DNS records (e.g., A, MX, TXT) to map out all available subdomains and mail servers.

###### &#x20;    > Wappalyzer: Analyzes a live website instantly to report what programming languages, CMS platforms, and web servers it runs on.

###### 

##### \# Why its important ?

###### &#x20;Ans)1. Minimizes Blind Spots: It reveals the entire "attack surface." Organizations often forget about old, forgotten servers or test websites, 

###### &#x20;                              which are exactly what hackers look for.

###### 

###### &#x20;    2. Prevents Detection: Doing deep passive recon allows testers to find structural flaws without triggering security alarms or getting their  

###### &#x20;                            IP addresses blocked early on.

###### 

###### &#x20;    3. Saves Time and Resources: Instead of randomly trying every hack, accurate recon allows professionals to pinpoint the exact vulnerability 

###### &#x20;                                 and plan a precise, efficient attack vector.

###### 

###### &#x20;    4. Prevents Collateral Damage: Knowing the exact blueprint of a network ensures that testing tools do not accidentally crash a company's 

###### &#x20;                                   production systems or delete vital data.



###### &#x20;    5. Improves Threat Intelligence: It helps security teams understand what an actual malicious attacker can see from the outside, allowing 

###### &#x20;                                     them to block that visibility before a breach happens.
